# Murlan Landscape Table — Design Audit

## Overall Assessment

The landscape table is built on a genuinely good idea — a single fixed-aspect canvas where everything is positioned in `%` and sized in `cqw` — and most of the chrome (top-bar buttons, seat names, card faces) faithfully follows it. The craft is real: there are deal/throw/finish animations, safe-area handling, a touch-target floor, and reduced-motion support already wired in. The problem is that the system is inconsistently applied and over-tuned for exactly one screen ratio: the felt is off-brand (blue/green instead of obsidian-and-gold), several high-stakes elements (timer, hand deal, functional countdowns) silently break the rules they were built around, and the layout only "fits" at precisely 2.17:1 — on the majority of real phones it letterboxes with cards floating over a black bar. The net effect is a table that looks polished in screenshots at the design ratio but reads as cramped, off-brand, and occasionally broken on actual devices.

## 🔴 High

- **Felt is off-brand: blue/green instead of obsidian-and-gold** (color-contrast) — The brand is explicitly "obsidian & gold" but the default `--felt` is casino green (`#1c6b3e`) and equipped cosmetics include saturated blues (`felt_sapphire #18306e`, `felt_midnight`). The largest surface on screen fights the gold chrome, so gold stops reading as intentional. *Pse:* you can't be an obsidian-and-gold product when the centrepiece is royal blue — it looks like two design languages stitched together, and the coherent `felt_obsidian` exists but isn't the default so most players never see it. *Rregullimi:* make `felt_obsidian` (or a near-black green-tinted felt) the default `--felt`; desaturate/darken the cosmetic blues to subtle tints over a near-black base; lean the gold ring harder so gold stays the hero on every felt. [index.css:26, :542-547; TableView.tsx:176]

- **Layout only fits at 2.17:1 — cards/buttons float over a black letterbox bar** (responsive) — The canvas is hard-`contain`ed to `1280/590` (2.169:1). Most phones in landscape are narrower (iPhone SE 1.78, many Androids 2.0), so the canvas is width-constrained and shorter than the screen, leaving black bars top and bottom. The hand (`top:65%`, bottom-clipped) and the Pas/Luaj buttons (`cy 81%`) then float mid-screen above a dead strip. *Pse:* the entire "cards clipped at the screen's bottom edge" intent depends on the canvas filling the height, which it does NOT on the majority of phone ratios — letterbox bars around a live game table read as broken. *Rregullimi:* either let the canvas be `100dvh × 100vw` with clamped `%`/`cqmin` positioning (accept minor horizontal crop), or keep `contain` but anchor the hand and action buttons to the true viewport bottom (`bottom: env(safe-area-inset-bottom)`, outside the aspect box). At minimum paint the letterbox bars as felt/obsidian. [index.css:724-728, :833-844, :900-909]

- **Player's own hand overlaps the felt bottom and the pile** (table-layout) — The felt bottom is at `60%` (354px); the hand starts at `top:65%` (only ~29px below) and is bottom-anchored with very tall cards (`CARD_W≤116 × 1.82 ≈ 211px`). A selected card lifts another 32px, so card tops reach ~330px — above the felt bottom and into the pile zone. *Pse:* this is the single most cramped relationship on the table — when you select cards to play, their raised edges visually merge with the pile you're trying to beat; there's no neutral band between "my cards" and "the table." *Rregullimi:* buy vertical separation — reduce felt height to ~44% and/or nudge it up, OR cap the landscape card height harder (`ratio ~1.6` or `CARD_W≤104`) so the lifted fan tops out around y~360 with a real gutter below the pile. [Hand.tsx:79,95; index.css:833-843 vs :788-791, :800-812]

- **Two-char ranks ("10", "JK") get clipped on a full hand — the exact bug the redesign claimed to fix** (cards) — On a 13-14 card hand the per-card step collapses to fit the zone, so the visible sliver is only ~14-22px while the rank token `.gc-r` (22cqw, ~25px) plus a two-glyph "10"/"JK" needs ~26-30px — the trailing digit/letter is covered by the next card. *Pse:* the whole thin-tall redesign was justified as making the full rank (incl. "10") always visible; it fails at the worst case, and a partially-covered "1" reading as a stray stroke is a real misread in a money game. *Rregullimi:* clamp the step to a floor (`step = max(CARD_W*0.20, fitStep)`) and let the fan scroll/scale instead of overlapping below the "10"-safe sliver; tuck the index to `left:3cqw` and drop `gc-r` to ~19-20cqw. Test specifically with a 14-card hand containing 10 and a joker. [Hand.tsx:79-81; index.css:849]

- **Top opponent seat collides with the top bar** (table-layout) — The top seat is centered at `cy 12%` (~70.8px) and renders its mini card-fan + green "(N)" count ABOVE the avatar, landing at ~y 35-60px — right under the top bar's right-side cluster (timer + chat/emoji, 5cqw≈64px). *Pse:* two HUD layers overlapping is a hard readability failure — the most-glanced number on the table (how many cards the leader holds) gets fouled by the chat/emoji buttons. Seats should never enter the chrome band. *Rregullimi:* lower the top seat to `cy ~16-18%`, or for the `top` placement render the fan+count to the side/below the avatar (you already special-case `top` for the led-last badge); cap topbar to ~7% and start seats no higher than 16%. [TableView.tsx:60; SeatBadge.tsx:87-91 vs index.css:742-746]

- **Pas and Luaj are the same size and shape — no primary/secondary hierarchy** (buttons) — Both action buttons are identical 12cqw circles at the bottom corners, same radius/font/shadow; the only difference is fill (Luaj gold, Pas a transparent ghost with a 1px hairline). *Pse:* mirror-imaged round twins give no glance hierarchy — the player can't tell which is "commit," and a ghost outline on busy felt reads as disabled/decorative. In a real-money game the primary action must dominate. *Rregullimi:* keep Luaj gold-filled and slightly larger (13-14cqw) with the gold glow; make Pas a solid dark-glass/obsidian disc with a gold hairline (~11cqw) so it reads as a real secondary button, not an outline. Differentiate by size + fill weight, not transparency. [index.css:900-909; Controls.tsx:60-65]

- **Top-bar icon buttons render well under the 44px touch minimum** (buttons) — `.tv-ls .tvc-topbar .iconbtn` sets `5cqw` with `min-width:0; min-height:0`, which renders ~37px once the canvas is scaled for a phone AND explicitly defeats the global `@media (hover:none){.iconbtn{44px}}` safeguard via higher specificity. *Pse:* WCAG 2.5.5 / Apple HIG want ≥44px; these chat/emoji buttons sit by the notch, are spaced only 1.5% apart, and the code clearly tried to guarantee 44px then silently undid it. *Rregullimi:* never zero `min-width/min-height`; enlarge to ~6.5-7cqw and/or add an invisible hit area, increase inter-button gap, and verify the rendered px at real device scale, not full-canvas px. [index.css:754-755 defeats :955]

- **Played card runs two conflicting entrance animations at once (pop + throwin)** (effects) — The newest pile layer wrapper gets `animate-pop` (scale, 180ms) while every card inside independently runs `throwin` (fly/overshoot/rotate, 360ms). Parent and child transforms multiply and the two timelines finish at different times, so the card double-bounces and stutters. *Pse:* two un-coordinated entrance animations on nested elements is the classic source of jank — the eye reads one motion but the geometry is the product of two. *Rregullimi:* pick ONE entrance — remove `animate-pop` from the top pile layer and let `throwin` carry it, or restrict `animate-pop` to already-settled older layers only. Align the start transform/opacity so they can't stack. [index.css:597-603; Pile.tsx:38]

- **Local player's hand has no deal-in animation — `dealAnimate` is a dead prop** (effects) — `dealAnimate` is passed to `<Hand>` and declared in props, `CardView` supports `dealDelayMs`, and `dealfly`/`dealIn` keyframes exist — but `Hand` never reads the prop, so the player's 13-14 cards blink into existence at full size while opponents get a shuffle splash. *Pse:* dealing is the single biggest "feel" beat of a card game and every other surface here has a polished entrance; the hand silently popping in is a glaring inconsistency at the most important moment. *Rregullimi:* when `dealAnimate` is true on a fresh deal, pass a staggered `dealDelayMs={i * ~35}` (or apply the existing keyframes) to each `CardView`, and ensure it never re-fires on reorder/selection. [Hand.tsx:36,189; declared :11, passed TableView.tsx:404]

- **Opponent seats mix three accent colours (gold + blue + green) at once** (color-contrast) — Each seat paints a blue idle ring, a neon-green "(N)" count, a blue partner name, a blue "led last" badge, and a gold turn ring — simultaneously. *Pse:* three competing hues on one tiny element destroys the obsidian-and-gold identity and creates noise where the eye should only need to find "whose turn is it" (gold); green/blue are also semantically loaded (success/info) but used decoratively here. *Rregullimi:* collapse the seat palette to gold + neutral — idle ring = neutral hairline, turn = gold (keep), led-last = muted gold/amber outline, count = cream/`txt-hi`. Reserve real hue only for the active-turn state. [index.css:404, :409, :466, :468-469]

- **Blue chrome disappears on the blue felt (non-text contrast fail)** (color-contrast) — The idle avatar ring is `--blue #3f6fd6` at only 2px on the canvas, and the "led last" badge is solid blue; on `felt_sapphire`/`felt_midnight` these sit on a same-hue blue field with almost no luminance separation. *Pse:* blue-on-blue at ~36px avatar size makes seat status effectively invisible, failing WCAG 1.4.11 (3:1 non-text contrast) — players can't read who's active or who led. *Rregullimi:* stop using `--blue` for status against the felt; give idle rings a light/neutral colour with a dark outline halo so they read on ANY felt, make "led last" a gold-bordered chip, and bump the ring to ~2.5-3cqw equivalent. [index.css:404, :824, :469, :544/:547]

- **Seat status / team labels are pinned at 11px and don't scale with the canvas** (typography) — Every seat element was migrated to `cqw` (name 1.7cqw, count 1.8cqw, lastp 1.4cqw) except the status line ("Ka pasuar"/"Radha"/"Larguar") and the 2v2 team tag, which are hard `text-[11px]` with no `.tv-ls` override. *Pse:* the entire `.tv-ls` system promises all type is a `%` of the canvas; a fixed 11px next to a `1.7cqw` name means their relative size flips between big and small phones — fragile hierarchy, exactly what the `cqw` migration was meant to kill. *Rregullimi:* give the status a real class (e.g. `seat-status`) instead of inline `text-[11px]`, and add `.tv-ls .tvc-seat` overrides expressing both in cqw (~1.4cqw). [SeatBadge.tsx:79,103; missing override in index.css ~825-829]

- **Top bar mixes safe-area px insets with %-based content — overflows on notched phones** (responsive) — `.tvc-topbar` uses `left/right/top: max(2%, env(safe-area-inset-*))`, so on a notched iPhone in landscape the ~44-50px insets shrink usable width ~100px, but the content (Largohu + multi-seat score on the left, timer + 💬 + 😊 on the right) is sized in cqw assuming near-full width. The left and right groups can collide or the score slides under the Dynamic Island; the fixed `9%` bar height is already tight. *Pse:* safe-area insets are absolute px while contents scale in cqw — the two don't track, so the layout breaks specifically on the notched devices the app forces into landscape. Score/leave controls vanishing under the notch is functional, not cosmetic. *Rregullimi:* give the left/right groups `min-w-0` + `max-width` budgets and let the score `truncate`; reserve the safe-area gutter on the canvas-wrap padding (not each absolute child) so the `%` grid stays constant; add a `min-height: ~40px` floor to the bar. [index.css:742-752; TableView.tsx:329-346]

## 🟡 Medium

- **Timer pill is sized in fixed px while the rest of the bar scales in cqw** (buttons/table-layout/responsive) — `TurnTimer` uses Tailwind `px-2.5 py-1 text-xs`; only the font is caught by the topbar's `text-xs→1.7cqw` rule, so padding stays a fixed 10/4px while neighbours scale. The spectator pill (`px-2 py-1`) has the same problem, and a fixed `py-1` height can exceed the 9% bar and overlap the felt. *Pse:* mixing fixed-px components into a fully cqw-scaled canvas breaks the proportional consistency that is this layout's core rule — and the countdown is the highest-stakes info (a missed turn auto-passes a paid hand), yet it's the least unified element. *Rregullimi:* add `.tv-ls .tvc-topbar` overrides for the timer + spectator pills (padding in cqh/cqw, emoji font-size in cqw, height capped to the bar), mirroring the `.btn`/`.iconbtn` treatment so all controls scale together. [TurnTimer.tsx:16-21; TableView.tsx:351-360; index.css:767-784]

- **Side seat name labels overrun the felt rail** (table-layout) — Side seats sit at `cx 15%/86%`; the avatar (5cqw) clears the felt but the name label (`max-width:16cqw`≈205px, center-aligned) stretches so its inner edge lands on the felt rail (felt spans 22-78%). *Pse:* a name bleeding over the wooden rail onto the green felt reads as a layering bug and muddies the table silhouette — seat pods should sit cleanly in the gutters. *Rregullimi:* shrink the side-seat name cap to ~11-12cqw and/or move side seats out to `cx ~11%/89%`; clamping the label width is the cheapest fix since only the label overflows. [TableView.tsx:61-62; index.css:825 vs :788-790]

- **Felt is undersized — wasted green interior, cramped rim** (table-layout) — The felt is only 56%×48% with a small betting ring in the middle, while seats are jammed at the edges (`cx 15%/86%, cy 12%`) and the hand is pushed off the bottom. Space is generous where nothing happens and scarce where it's needed. *Pse:* the visual grid allocates room backwards — interior felt padding gets it, seat gutters and the hand band don't. *Rregullimi:* either grow the felt (~62% wide) so seats move off the rim, or shrink the ring's internal inset so the betting zone fills more felt and the surrounding margin shrinks; rebalance so gutters and hand band are the priority. [index.css:788-791, :796; TableView.tsx:60-62]

- **Pile history stack drifts up-left out of the betting ring** (table-layout) — Each earlier trick layer offsets by `translate(-back*11, -back*13)` for up to 6 layers (−55px/−65px), pushing the oldest layer's top to ~y96 — into the felt top inset, toward the top seat, and off the ring center. *Pse:* the gold ring is the designed focal point, but a multi-card history climbs out toward the top-left and risks overlapping the top opponent. *Rregullimi:* reduce per-layer offset (~6px/5px), cap history to 3-4 layers in landscape, and/or split the drift up AND down so the stack stays inside the ring. [Pile.tsx:14,42 vs index.css:796, :800-802]

- **Pile uses fixed-px offsets that don't scale with the canvas** (responsive) — The same `translate(-back*11px, -back*13px)` is fixed px while pile cards are sized in cqw (`8cqw`≈34px on a small canvas), so a 6-deep stack spreads ~55/65px — nearly two card-widths — and looks tidy on one device, scattered on another. *Pse:* mixing absolute-px geometry into a cqw-scaled canvas defeats the "everything is a % of the container" system. *Rregullimi:* express the offset relative to the canvas (e.g. `--pile-step: 1.3cqw` in the `.tv-ls` scope, or pass a canvas-derived step into the inline transform). [Pile.tsx:41; index.css:808-813]

- **Cards snap instead of sliding on reorder — `left` transition dropped** (effects) — `.hand-card` is styled to tween `left 0.18s` + `transform`, but every non-dragging card overrides this inline with `transition: 'transform .12s ease'`, which omits `left` — the exact property that changes on a drag-reorder. So neighbours jump instead of gliding. *Pse:* drag-to-reorder is a core interaction and the whole point of the `left` tween is that neighbours glide aside; snapping makes a built feature feel broken. *Rregullimi:* include `left` in the resting inline transition (`'left .18s ease, transform .12s ease'`); keep `transition:'none'` only on the card actively being dragged. [Hand.tsx:166 overriding index.css:509]

- **Old history cards re-fire `throwin` on every new play (index-based keys)** (effects) — The pile keys layers by array index while `throwin` applies to all `.pile-cards .gcard`; once `slice(-6)` starts shifting, the combo at a given index changes, so settled history cards remount and re-run the 360ms throw. *Pse:* background trick cards lurching back into motion on an unrelated play is the "things move when they shouldn't" class of jank. *Rregullimi:* key each layer by its content (joined cardKeys) so a shifting layer keeps identity and doesn't remount, and/or scope `throwin` to a `.is-new` top layer only. [Pile.tsx:32-50; index.css:603-608]

- **Reduced-motion kills the functional turn countdown** (effects) — The avatar turn-ring (`deplete`) and local turn bar (`deplete-bar`) are functional indicators driven by `animation-duration: <remaining>ms`, but the universal reduced-motion rule forces `0.001ms !important`, so they snap instantly to empty — falsely reading as "time's up." *Pse:* reduced-motion should remove decorative motion, not destroy a money-relevant indicator. *Rregullimi:* exempt the functional countdowns from the universal kill, or drive them from JS state like `TurnTimer` already does. [index.css:966-973 vs :428-434, :454-465]

- **Pas/Luaj have almost no press feedback** (effects) — Every other button presses with `translateY(0) scale(0.98)` + shadow, but the round Pas/Luaj force `:active` back to `translate(-50%,-50%) !important` to protect centering, leaving only a `brightness(1.15)` flash — nearly invisible on the already-bright gold. *Pse:* tactile press confirmation is essential on touch; the two most-pressed buttons getting the weakest feedback is a feel regression. *Rregullimi:* scale around the existing translate — `transform: translate(-50%,-50%) scale(.93) !important` on `:active`. [index.css:910-916]

- **No clear disabled state for Pas/Luaj in landscape** (buttons) — The only disabled cue is the base `opacity:0.45 + grayscale`, but in landscape the hint text and Clear button are hidden, so the buttons must carry the entire "can I act?" affordance — and a 45%-opacity grayscaled gold circle is barely distinguishable from active. *Pse:* players can't tell whether passing is even allowed (it's forbidden while leading), so it feels like a frozen UI. *Rregullimi:* add an explicit landscape disabled treatment distinct from the active fills; consider restoring a one-line reason when Pass is forbidden. [index.css:188, :898; Controls.tsx:60,63]

- **Largohu (forfeit) is styled identically to the cosmetic chat/emoji buttons** (buttons) — Largohu becomes a dark-glass pill with `--hairline-gold`; the chat/emoji `.iconbtn`s use the SAME gradient and SAME gold hairline, differing only in shape. Leaving a live paid match looks identical to "send an emoji." *Pse:* visual hierarchy should reflect consequence — a forfeit control sharing a look with throwaway buttons risks costly accidental taps. *Rregullimi:* differentiate Largohu with a subtle danger-tinted hairline or muted-red hover/press accent, plus extra spacing from the chat/emoji cluster; keep the confirm modal. [index.css:767-778 vs :754-761; TableView.tsx:764-780]

- **Hand cards show only a tiny top-left corner — the face is a blank cream slab** (cards) — Only `.gc-r` and `.gc-s` are restyled for the sliver; the centre suit `.gc-big` stays dead-centre (hidden under the right neighbour) and `.gc-br` lives in the clipped bottom. Each tall card presents only a small top-left mark on empty cream. *Pse:* real card affordance comes from redundant corner indices plus a body pip; this wastes the height the redesign added. *Rregullimi:* for `.tv-ls .tvc-hand`, move a secondary mark into the always-visible upper band — reposition/shrink `.gc-big` to upper-centre/left, or repurpose `.gc-br` as a second top-aligned index. [index.css:495-496, :835]

- **Red vs black joker distinguished by colour alone** (cards) — `suitSymbol` returns the same `★` and `rankText` returns `JK` for both jokers; only `color:var(--red)` vs ink differs. The red joker outranks the black, so these are functionally different cards separated only by hue — invisible to red-green colour-blind players. *Pse:* cards with different game power must differ by shape/label, not colour alone (WCAG 1.4.1); a joker misread is costly in a money game. *Rregullimi:* give them distinct marks/labels — `🃏` vs `★`, or `JKR`/`JKB`. [cards.ts:39-45; CardView.tsx:33-36]

- **Hand cards (1.82) and pile cards (1.4) are two different shapes on one table** (cards) — A card visibly changes proportion when it moves from your hand to the pile — both visible at once. *Pse:* a core object shouldn't change shape mid-flight; the mismatch undercuts the "thin+tall" identity. *Rregullimi:* unify the ratio — set pile height to `8cqw * 1.82` (or one shared CSS var/constant). [index.css:810 vs Hand.tsx:95]

- **Score, names and status sit at ~12px on small phones — below comfortable mobile floor** (typography) — Score (1.7cqw), names (1.7cqw), badges (1.4cqw / 11px) render ~11-13px at a ~780px canvas. *Pse:* 11-13px is the floor for arm's-length landscape text; the live score shouldn't sit at the bottom of legibility. *Rregullimi:* bump score+names to ~2.0-2.2cqw and lastp/status to ~1.6cqw, or `clamp(12px, 1.9cqw, 18px)`. [index.css:784, :825-827]

- **Avatar initials are too small for the disc (1.8cqw in a 5cqw circle)** (typography) — Monogram fills only ~36% of the avatar diameter. *Pse:* monograms read best at ~50-60% fill; at 36% the avatar looks empty. *Rregullimi:* raise initials to ~2.4-2.6cqw, font-weight 800. [index.css:824]

- **Name (Outfit) and count (Oswald) sit side-by-side in different typefaces** (typography) — The seat NAME inherits body Outfit while the adjacent "(N)" count is Oswald, 6px apart. *Pse:* the role split must be consistent; here the eye catches the mismatch where the labels touch. *Rregullimi:* display numerals/labels all Oswald; running text/names Outfit — make name+count share one family. [index.css:466 vs :467]

- **Switch dim state is a flat 40% opacity — looks broken, washes red pips to pink** (color-contrast) — Ineligible cards get a blanket `opacity:0.4` with no positive highlight on eligible ones; red suits go pink. *Pse:* flat global opacity reads as "disabled/loading," not "not pickable." *Rregullimi:* keep ineligible at ~0.55 with `grayscale`, and give ELIGIBLE cards a positive gold ring/glow. [Hand.tsx:168]

- **Maroon card backs clash with the cool felt and aren't in the palette** (color-contrast) — The default back / mini fans / shuffle splash are warm maroon; against blue felt this is a warm-on-cool clash. *Pse:* maroon is neither obsidian nor gold. *Rregullimi:* make the default back obsidian-and-gold (dark slate + gold pinstripe); keep maroon as an opt-in cosmetic. [index.css:474; CardView.tsx:50-51]

- **Neon-green seat count is low-contrast on green felt and off-palette** (color-contrast) — The "(N)" count uses `#7cffa8`; on green felt it's green-on-green, and reads like a debug colour. *Pse:* green-on-green is low contrast, and this count is one of the most-read numbers. *Rregullimi:* recolour to cream/gold with a dark halo; reserve neon green for a genuine last-card warning. [index.css:466, :826]

- **Landscape gate `max-height: 560px` strands mid-size devices** (responsive) — `ls` only triggers at landscape + `max-height:560px`; a device at 561-840px landscape falls back to the portrait flow while physically sideways. *Pse:* a single magic-px height creates ratio-blind dead zones. *Rregullimi:* gate on aspect-ratio; make portrait/landscape branches mutually exhaustive. [useForceLandscape.ts:23-26]

## 🟢 Low

- **Pile sits ~21px below the betting-ring center** — ring centers ~36% but pile is `cy 39.5%`. *Rregullimi:* set `.tvc-pile top` to ~36%. [index.css:800-802 vs :788-796]
- **Pile ranks clipped at 50% overlap** — pile inherits base `.gc-r left:11cqw` with no sliver-aware override. *Rregullimi:* reduce pile overlap to `-3cqw` or add a `.tv-ls .tvc-pile .gc-r` tuck. [index.css:808-813]
- **Selected card keeps `z-index = i`** — a lifted low card is still covered by its right neighbour. *Rregullimi:* `zIndex: isSel ? n + i : i`. [Hand.tsx:166]
- **Round Pas/Luaj get a rectangular/suppressed focus ring** — transform `!important` fights the focus shadow; global fallback uses `border-radius:6px`. *Rregullimi:* explicit circular focus-visible style. [index.css:915-916, :131-137]
- **Switch Konfirmo/Anulo are tiny pills with sub-44px targets** (`min-height:0`). *Rregullimi:* enforce a tap-size floor; Konfirmo dominant gold, Anulo solid secondary. [index.css:894 overriding :959]
- **Landscape hand rank sized off card WIDTH only** — `22cqw` of a narrow card reads small on a tall face. *Rregullimi:* bump to ~26-28cqw or size from `cqmin`/`cqh`. [index.css:849]
- **Score target is the least legible element of the score** (`text-xs` + `opacity-50`). *Rregullimi:* raise target opacity/size; the thing you race to shouldn't be faintest. [TableView.tsx:339,344]
- **Pas/Luaj labels can wrap in the circle** (no `white-space:nowrap`). *Rregullimi:* add `nowrap`, verify fit at 2.6cqw. [index.css:900-906]
- **Go-out finish stacks three transforms on one element** (`finish-pop` + `throwin` + `animate-pop`). *Rregullimi:* sequence/suppress so the slow-mo scale is the only motion. [TableView.tsx:520; index.css:614-619]
- **MMR delta and timer use raw Tailwind tokens instead of `--success`/`--danger`.** *Rregullimi:* route through tokens — one green, one red, app-wide. [TableView.tsx:706; TurnTimer.tsx:19]
- **Idle gold accents washed out** — resting table shows almost no gold (ring 0.45, watermark 0.08, logo hidden in landscape). *Rregullimi:* raise resting ring alpha (~0.6), add a thin gold rail + small wordmark. [index.css:376, :396, :797]
- **Selected-card lift is a fixed px on a container-derived card** — can intrude on felt on the shortest phones. *Rregullimi:* express `--card-lift` relative to card height (cqh). [Hand.tsx:166; index.css:501-506]
